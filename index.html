About me

I don't like to be put in a box. I'm a designer, an organizer, a problem-solver — and someone who genuinely believes that good design should work for everyone. My path into design wasn't linear. I started in advertising and post-production agencies, then spent 8+ years leading international projects in the social sector. That work taught me how to navigate complexity, listen carefully, and design for real humans in messy, imperfect situations.

In 2022 I found my way into digital product design — and it felt like things clicked into place. I see design as more than visual problem solving. It's about creating experiences that are intuitive, inclusive, and actually useful. Accessibility isn't a checkbox for me — it was shaped by years of working directly alongside people with disabilities, long before it became a legal requirement.

Outside of work: I climb, do yoga, engage in my community and recently started drawing fineline illustrations.

Why Me? My Key Strengths

15+ years across very different worlds

From advertising agencies and post-production to social enterprises, freelancing and B2B product teams. That breadth means I can manage complexity, explore different fields and bring structure without losing sight of the human in the middle.

Accessibility: an inclusive mindset from the beginning

Certified in WCAG 2.2 and shaped by eight years working directly alongside people with disabilities. For me inclusivity isn't something I add at the end. It's how I start.

A builder's mindset

Regardless of the task, I take ownership. I ask why. I bring structure and clarity and I'm not afraid to go the extra mile to make something genuinely good.

Designing with AI but consciously

Certified AI Manager with a critical perspective. I work with AI tools intentionally — embracing what genuinely improves the design process while keeping the human at the center.

Empathy as a working method

My top Clifton Strengths result is Empathy which honestly makes sense for this job. I want to understand people before I try to solve their problems. And it shows in how I conduct research, give feedback, and collaborate with others.

Fluent across disciplines

I'm comfortable in the room with engineers, PMs, and business stakeholders. I translate between worlds — turning research into priorities, and design decisions into language that lands.
